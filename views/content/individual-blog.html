Five Ways To Make It Easier For You To Brainstorm For Your Next Blog Post

So, we rocked at Social Media Week Mumbai this year too! Not only were we content partners with SMW, but we also hosted two sessions. A Talk on how to make your content work for your customers as well as the importance of publishing relevant content and ‘Think and Do Dum De Da’ – an interactive workshop where we discussed easy and fun ways to crack content briefs. And we are sure we were a hit, with the amount of response we got on Twitter – 105 Mentions, and 98 Followers in a single day!

Anyway, while at SMW 2015, we shared tips on brainstorming for content briefs. So, here we thought of sharing a few ways which could help you brainstorm for your next blog post instead!

  • 1. Tell Your Story

    People love that. Any article that has a personal touch, people love reading. Moreover, it helps you connect with your readers because chances are that, they’ve been through something similar. Talk about how you started and what has changed since then. Or even whatever you’ve learnt since then. It can also prove to be a conversation starter, what with people commenting and sharing their stories.

  • 2. Give Answers

    Most people are looking for exactly that, on the internet. We do. We are sure you do too. Everyone wants answers to their questions and doubts. So give it to them without another thought. Say, kitchen hacks or organizing tips. You can also put up a ‘How To’ blog post or review movies, books or even products for that matter. There is always something you know, which you think is common knowledge. But there is always someone who is looking for exactly that. It may be a recipe which is so common to your culture, that you would wonder why you should be writing about it. But what if, someone in the other part of the world wants to learn to cook it?

  • 3. Observations

    It’s been told and retold, but always keep your eyes and ears open, no matter how ordinary it sounds. Observe everything that goes on around you. It doesn’t have to be something unique. Even description of a busy Railway platform, sometimes sounds interesting. Just carry a notepad with you, or even your smartphone will do. The next time you’re travelling, or waiting at a café for your friends to arrive, you know how to keep yourself busy!

  • 4. Something Old, Something New

    Your older blog can actually give you something to write about for your next blog post. You can write a follow-up blog if you have something more to add to it. Or take an old image you’ve clicked and turn it into a photo blog. This can go on!

  • 5. Keep an eye on the trends

    For many, trends act like writing prompts. For one, you get cues from what’s trending. Secondly, you know what people are discussing. That way, you know what people are interested in and what they are looking for right now. The best way to look for trends is to keep going through Google Trends, or even Twitter Trends.
